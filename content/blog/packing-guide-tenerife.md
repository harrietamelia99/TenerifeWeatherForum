Packing for Tenerife trips up more UK visitors than you'd think. The island's extraordinary microclimate system means you might be wearing a t-shirt on the beach at 10am and wishing you'd brought a fleece at the top of Teide three hours later. This guide covers everything you need, tailored by season.

## Year-Round Essentials

These go in the bag regardless of when you're visiting:

**Sun protection**
- SPF 50+ sunscreen - and plenty of it. UV in Tenerife is intense year-round, routinely reaching index 8 or 9 in summer and 6–7 even in January. Most people bring far less than they need. Buy a large bottle before you go - it's noticeably more expensive at resort pharmacies and supermarkets
- Polarised sunglasses - the light off the Atlantic is particularly intense
- A wide-brimmed hat or cap, especially if you're spending time outdoors or hiking

**Clothing basics**
- Light layers rather than heavy items. Tenerife evenings are warm but breezy - a light cardigan or linen shirt is usually enough
- At least one warmer layer (light fleece or jacket) if you're planning any altitude excursions
- Comfortable walking shoes. The volcanic terrain on excursions can be uneven

**Health and admin**
- Your GHIC card (Global Health Insurance Card, replaced the EHIC post-Brexit) - this covers emergency healthcare costs in Spain
- Travel insurance documents - print them, don't rely on your phone battery
- Type C/F plug adapters - Spain uses 2-pin round plugs. UK flat-pin chargers won't work without an adapter. Take two

**Practical items**
- Reusable water bottle - tap water in Tenerife is technically drinkable but locals and most visitors drink bottled water. A large refillable bottle keeps plastic use down and saves money
- Small day bag or backpack for excursions
- Lip balm with SPF - easily forgotten and the sun dries your lips quickly

## What to Pack by Season

### Summer: June to September

The south bakes in summer. Temperatures regularly reach 30–33°C in July and August, and the UV index can hit 11 (extreme) at midday. Pack as light as possible.

- Lightweight linen or cotton clothing - synthetics feel suffocating in this heat
- Loose-fitting shirts and dresses - breathable is everything
- A light cover-up for evenings on the promenade
- Flip flops or sandals for beach and resort use
- Closed-toe shoes for any walking or day trips

**Note on summer packing:** Even in summer, bring one warmer layer if you're planning to go to Teide. The national park sits at 2,000 metres and temperatures there are 10–15°C cooler than the coast.

### Autumn: October and November

October and November are transition months. The south stays warm (23–26°C) with good sunshine. The north becomes more unsettled, with increasing cloud and some rain.

- Light summer clothing still works for the south
- Add a waterproof layer - a lightweight packable rain jacket takes up almost no space
- One warmer layer for evenings
- Closed shoes for any northern or mountain excursions

### Winter: December to February

Winter in the south is genuinely mild - 19–22°C is typical, and on good days it can reach 24°C. You won't be lying on a sunlounger in shorts all day, but you'll be comfortable in light clothing by day. Evenings are cooler, typically 14–16°C, and a proper jacket is needed.

The north in winter is noticeably cooler and can feel quite chilly - temperatures of 15–17°C with regular cloud and some rain. Pack accordingly if you're planning time in Puerto de la Cruz or the Orotava Valley.

- Pack as if for a mild UK spring/early summer - layers rather than one type of clothing
- A proper jacket or mid-layer for evenings (not just a cardigan)
- Waterproof layer - rain is more likely in winter, even in the south
- Jeans or trousers for evenings - you'll feel underdressed in shorts

**Teide in winter:** Mount Teide can have snow from November through March. If you're visiting the national park in winter, proper warm and waterproof clothing is essential. The summit temperature can be well below freezing.

### Spring: March to May

Spring is Tenerife at its best. Temperatures are warm without being extreme, UV is building, and the island looks its most spectacular - particularly in the north and the Orotava Valley.

- Light clothing for daytime - temperatures reach 22–26°C in the south
- One or two warmer layers for evenings
- Light waterproof in case - spring can see more variable weather than summer
- Good walking shoes if you're planning any hiking

## If You're Planning to Hike

Tenerife has excellent hiking, particularly in the Anaga peninsula, the Teide National Park, and the Orotava Valley. If hiking is on the agenda:

- **Proper hiking boots or trail shoes** - volcanic rock is abrasive on trainers
- **Poles** if you have them - useful on loose volcanic scree
- **At least 2 litres of water per person** - there are few water sources on most trails
- **Sun hat and SPF** - shade is limited on many routes
- **Warm layers for Teide** - even in summer, the summit area can be cold and windy

## What You Don't Need to Bring

- **Lots of formal clothing** - Tenerife is casual. Even the better restaurants don't require smart dress
- **Heavy denim** - jeans feel heavy and take ages to dry. Lightweight chinos or travel trousers are much more practical
- **Expensive jewellery** - not because Tenerife is unsafe, but because it's unnecessary and easier to leave at home
- **Excessive medication** - Spanish pharmacies are excellent and well-stocked. Most UK over-the-counter medications have Spanish equivalents easily available
